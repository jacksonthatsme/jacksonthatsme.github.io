Kickstarter
Brand & Product
2017
Most recently I have been working at the intersection of brand and product, helping Kickstarter to effectively communicate its mission throughout the web experience. While the efforts are ongoing, our goal has been to frame the diverse pool of projects funding at any given moment into a cohesive narrative that broadcasts our values and celebrates our creators, while providing relevant pathways into our discovery and creation channels.

Show and Tell
We saw a prime opportunity to use the homepage as an outlet to educate about the platform, and build report with the creative community. Our hypothesis was that a more opinionated, editorialized presentation of the material would elevate our content and reinforce our reputation as a home for creators. We tested these assumptions in a week long GV Design Sprint.

Focus on stories
Following an independent study detailing the economic impact of Kickstarter over the last 7 years, we took the opportunity to pair the statistics with narratives about the people behind the projects and the communities that connected with them.
Honor the content
It became really important to showcase these stories with the same amount of care and attention that the creators themselves put into their projects. We developed a pattern that gave each vignette it’s own singular moment, and promoted exploring through all of the stories by placing them in a continuous experience.

Concept car Kickstarter
This nearly year long process became an exhaustive reimagining of the entire platform: as a team we crafted an entirely new UX pattern, overhauled the UI to fit, and introduced animation principles.
Show the crowd
A main focus of the work was to provide more opportunities throughout the browse experience to contextualize projects. Concepts like badging and project collections were investigated as a way to differentiate projects in quality and in relevance to the user. Social influence was emphasized wherever available.
Keeping it smooth
We made a conscious effort to ensure that there was a consistent and clear flow throughout the updated experience. To do so, we made motion prototyping a core part of our design process.
